In the coastal and hill terrain of New Plymouth, slope stability is governed by the region’s volcanic geology, weathered ash layers, and frequent rainfall. Our work addresses erosion-prone coastal cliffs, cut-and-fill failures, and deep-seated landslides under the New Zealand Geotechnical Society guidelines and the Building Code clause B1. We begin with rigorous slope stability analysis to quantify factors of safety, then integrate active/passive anchor design where reinforcement must counteract tension cracks or weak tephra seams.

Typical projects range from rural subdivision platforms to road cuttings along steep coastal corridors, often requiring combined retention and drainage solutions. For sites with limited setback, we develop retaining wall design that works with the slope rather than against it, ensuring long-term performance against cyclic wetting and seismic demands.

Slope stability is a critical consideration for any development on the varied terrain of New Plymouth. Our specialised geotechnical services cover the full spectrum of slope assessment, from initial risk identification to the design of robust stabilisation measures. The region's unique geology, dominated by the volcanic lahar deposits of the Ring Plain and deeply weathered sedimentary rocks of the Waitara Fault Zone, creates complex ground conditions that demand a thorough understanding of local soil and rock behaviour. We guide every project through the relevant New Zealand regulatory framework, with investigations specifically designed to satisfy the requirements of the New Plymouth District Council, building on the principles of NZGS guidelines and the compliance pathways of B1/VM4 of the New Zealand Building Code. A foundational step in any slope assessment is a targeted geotechnical investigation, which uncovers the subsurface profile that dictates stability.

Our methodology for slope analysis strictly adheres to established New Zealand standards to deliver verifiable, defensible results. We characterise the critical shear strength of the soils through advanced In-Situ and comprehensive laboratory analysis. A key part of this process is the precise determination of index properties, including Atterberg limits to understand the plasticity of fine-grained tephra-derived soils, and a full grain size analysis to classify the material. For a direct, high-resolution stratigraphic profile, particularly through variable volcanic sequences, we deploy the CPT (Cone Penetration Test). All data is then synthesised using industry-accepted limit equilibrium methods to calculate Factors of Safety that clearly demonstrate compliance with acceptable risk criteria for static and seismic conditions as per NZGS guidelines.

We support a broad range of slope-related projects across the Taranaki landscape, from the coastal cliffs of Paritutu to the rolling hills of the hinterland. Our work is integral to residential subdivisions requiring a safe platform on a cut-and-fill bench, where we verify engineered fill density using the field density test (sand cone method). We also investigate instability in established areas, such as the creeping slopes in the Frankleigh Park area, and design soil nail or anchored retaining solutions for new commercial builds in the CBD. For critical rural infrastructure, we assess the stability of deep cuts for road realignments and evaluate the risk of debris flows on pastoral slopes, providing foundations design for structures located within areas of potential instability.

Our process converts complex geological risk into a clear, manageable project pathway. A typical slope assessment commences with a desktop review and a site-specific investigation, followed by laboratory testing to define the engineering properties of the soil. We then perform rigorous stability modelling, culminating in a comprehensive report that is more than just a data summary; it is a practical tool for design. This report provides interpreted ground models, calculated Factors of Safety, and actionable recommendations for earthworks, drainage, and any necessary structural reinforcement. The ultimate value we deliver is absolute confidence for our clients—whether they are a homeowner, a developer, or a civil contractor—that their New Plymouth project is grounded in a thorough understanding of the site's geology and meets every requirement of the New Zealand regulatory environment.
